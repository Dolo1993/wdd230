Target Audience

As a graphic designer target audience include: 1. Local businesses and business owners: Your designs will be primarily focused on promoting the chamber to this target audience, encouraging them to become members and participate in chamber events. 2. Community leaders: Your designs may also be directed towards community leaders, such as government officials and representatives from civic organizations, to promote the chamber's mission and goals and encourage their involvement. 3. Potential members and general public: Your designs will also be aimed at individuals and families in the community, encouraging them to support local businesses and become members of the chamber. 4. Visitors: If the chamber also promotes tourism in the area, your designs will be aimed at visitors, encouraging them to visit and support local businesses. Overall, your target audience will be diverse, and it will depend on the specific focus and mission of the chamber, as well as the specific initiatives and events it supports.

Two Personas

1. The Small Business Owner: This persona is likely a busy individual who owns and operates a small business in the local community. They are looking for ways to promote their business and connect with other local business owners. They may be looking for resources and networking opportunities offered by the chamber. This persona may be looking for simple, clean and clear designs that are easy to read and understand, and that provide them with the information they need in a quick and efficient manner.

2. The Community Leader: This persona is likely a busy individual who is involved in community organizations, such as government or civic groups. They are interested in supporting the local business community and promoting economic development. They may be looking for information about the chamber's mission and goals, as well as details about upcoming events and initiatives. This persona may be looking for designs that are professional and polished, and that provide a clear and compelling message about the chamber's value and impact on the community.

Scenarios

1. A small business owner hires a graphic designer to create a new logo and visual identity for their company. The designer works with the business owner to understand their brand and target audience, and creates a range of design options for them to choose from. Once a final design is selected, the designer may also create various marketing materials such as business cards, brochures, and a website to match the new visual identity.

2. A marketing department at a larger corporation hires a graphic designer to create graphics and layouts for a new advertising campaign. The designer works with the marketing team to understand the campaign's goals and target audience, and creates a range of design options for them to choose from. Once a final design is selected, the designer may also create various print and digital ads to match the campaign.

2. A graphic designer starts their own freelance business, offering design services to a variety of clients. They may work with small businesses, larger corporations, or other freelancers and agencies. They may create a wide range of design materials including logos, websites, brochures, and advertising campaigns. They may also work with clients to understand their needs and goals, and create design options for them to choose from.
